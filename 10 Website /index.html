Business analysis

  • Enrolling now
  • Location Online
  • WEEKLY WORKLOAD 10 hours
  • DURATION 2,5–3 months

The internship is designed for those who want to work in IT or are already in the industry and want to change direction. After an internship, analysts are undergraduates and graduates, designers and support specialists, as well as people not from IT at all, for example, from banks and logistics.

The main requirement is English language level B2 and above.

The basis of the internship is the practical part, which is as close as possible to the real work of a business analyst at Mercury.

On an internship

In a short time, you will be able to master all the main aspects of business analysis - from getting to know the customer of a potential project and ending with the preparation of requirements for the final product; design a ready-made interface and conduct its demo presentation; improve business writing skills and English; learn about functional and non-functional requirements and prepare a specification based on them.

The internship will take place in 2 stages. At the first - lectures and a lot of practice. The second will be individual work with a curator on educational projects with real tasks from the world of commercial development.

Consider Topics

  1. Identification and analysis of requirements
  2. Communication with the customer
  3. Interface design
  4. Preparation and support of project documentation
  5. Non-functional requirements and technical limitations

Conditions

The duration of the internship is 2.5–3 months. Employment from 10 hours a week, but more if you have a desire and free time. The schedule is flexible and takes into account your personal schedule.

We pay scholarships to successful interns, and we invite the best ones to work at Mercury.

How to get an internship

  1. Please respond via the form below. We are preliminarily accepting applications until February 18, but if there are more than usual candidates, the recruitment may close earlier.
  2. Candidates who have passed the initial selection will be invited to the first lecture.
  3. Participation is competitive: during the entire internship, curators select participants based on completed tasks.

.NET Bootcamp

  • Enrolling now
  • Location Online
  • WEEKLY WORKLOAD 10 hours
  • DURATION 2,5–3 months

.NET Bootcamp is an intensive course from leading Mercury backenders for those who want to learn server-side development on the .NET platform.

The bootcamp is designed for guys who already have some work experience.

The program consists of two stages:

  1. We conduct online meetups, issue and check assignments. Based on the results of the work, we select the best for the next stage.
  2. We get together in teams and develop web applications under the guidance of mentors. We invite the most successful candidates to work at Mercury.

Consider Topics:

  • The software development process in Mercury.
  • Git version control system.
  • Entity Framework.
  • Best practices
  • Web application architecture.
  • ASP.NET Web API.
  • Automated testing.

Requirements

  • Strong knowledge of C# syntax.
  • Understanding of OOP principles.
  • Ability to write SQL queries.
  • Knowledge of English A2 and above to understand the documentation.

Will be a plus

  • Experience in real projects.
  • Experience with version control systems.
  • Understanding REST API.
  • Understanding the principles of asynchronous programming (async/await, Tasks).
  • Experience in using LINQ, ORM systems.
  • Understanding how Dependency Injection works.

Conditions

Duration: 2.5–3 months of lectures and 1 month of practice. Employment from 10 hours a week, but more if you have a desire and free time. The schedule is flexible and takes into account your personal schedule.

We pay scholarships to successful participants, and we invite the best ones to work at Mercury.

How to get to the bootcamp

  1. Please respond via the form below. We are preliminarily accepting applications until February 14, but if there are more than usual candidates, the recruitment may close earlier.
  2. We will send a test task to those who pass the initial selection.
  3. The best candidates will receive an invitation.

Basics of QA

  • Enrolling now
  • Location Online
  • WEEKLY WORKLOAD 10–20 hours
  • DURATION 1–1.5 months

During this course trainees will be introduced to Quality Assurance concepts, will try on the role of a QA Engineer, and will learn the software development process from the inside out.

The job of a QA engineer is to ensure that the developed software works as expected and that the users are satisfied.

Who this internship is for:

IT students and technical specialists with previous experience
This internship will suit IT students and graduates, as well as IT specialists already working in related fields, who would like to try their hand at testing as part of the software development process.

Graduates of IT courses and internships
A degree in IT is not required if you completed IT courses or internships.

Those who are proficient in English
We welcome candidates with a good command of the English language, such as English language students and graduates, who are looking to make a career in the highly competitive IT field.

This internship will cover the basics of QA: how to handle bugs, different types of testing, how to plan testing and prepare test cases. The course will include theory (lectures and self-study materials) and hands-on experience, where you will be working on individual assignments.

All assignments will be checked and graded by internship supervisors, who will explain any shortcomings and make their recommendations. An Internship Completion Certificate will be awarded to the interns who have scored enough points for all completed assignments.

List of topics:

  1. Bug reporting
  2. Types of testing
  3. Requirement analysis and test planning
  4. Preparing test cases
  5. Test design
  6. Working with testing tools and services
  7. Platform-specific testing

Requirements

A degree in IT (completed or in progress) will be a plus, but is not required.
What is required: to be technically minded, reasonably perfectionist, attentive to detail, meticulous, and good at organizing data.

Our history

Mercury Academy enrolled its first students back in 2012, and we were very impressed with the outcome.

After the end of the course, we selected the top 8 students out of 134, and invited them to join our company. In no time at all, Academy graduates became lead developers in our company, and some of them are still working at Mercury.

Change of format

Somewhere around 2014, we decided to slightly change the learning format to give more emphasis to self-learning and take a more individual approach to our trainees through dedicated supervisors (the role taken on by our very best engineers).

After completing their training, the most successful trainees will be invited to join our team. Hours are flexible: we can work around your schedule.

We keep in touch with all our former trainees, and, based on the information we have, those who did not receive a job offer from us for some reason, in most cases found work at other IT companies within 1-2 months, without any problems.

FAQ

How long is the training?

As a rule, two to three months. It really depends on how much time you can afford to spend on it.

How is the training organized? Will I be able to balance it with my classes?

The training mostly relies on self-study. You will be assigned a supervisor who will be helping you to learn theory and will check your practical tasks. Most of the training can be done from home, with the exception of iOS training (if you don’t have a Mac at home, you’ll have to come to our lab). You can do the training in your free time. The only thing you need to schedule in advance is your meetings with your supervisor. On the average, the training will take 10 hours a week.

If I train at your company, will I be required to work for you?

Training at our company does not mean that you have to work for us.

Will I get a job at your company if I successfully complete my training?

We cannot guarantee that you will get a job. Everything will depend on the situation. If we have an opening at the junior level, candidates who successfully completed our training will be given a priority. However, if we don’t have any openings at the time, we cannot guarantee that we will hire you.

Will I receive any document to confirm that I successfully completed the training?

Yes, you will. We give certificates to all trainees who finish the program.
